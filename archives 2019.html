Masese 1 Youth Cage Fish Farmerâ€™s Project


A team of youths in Masese jinja have abandoned illegal methods of fishing and resulted in to a rather profitable and legal agribusiness activity called cage fish farming. This involves raising fish commercially in an enclosure known as cages. The youths acquire fish fingerlings for Nile Tilapia at a very tender stage weighing around 0.3-1g and they raise it for a period of 9-10 moths. They average weight harvested at this stage is 400-500g Fish farming has played a very big role in creating employment for these youths in the process improving their livelihood and income generation. The national economic development has been boosted as well as providing nutrition to the surrounding population. Cage fish farming activity is targeting the growing number of unemployed youth fishermen who are currently unemployed after the government deployed the army to control illegal fishing practices resulting into fish depletion and low fish exports thereby affecting the national economy. The project beneficiaries have been the unemployed youth who have acquired technical skills and in return have become supervisors, monitors and instructors of cage fish farmers. The skilled have skilled the unskilled thereby creating an environment of growth and development in this area.
